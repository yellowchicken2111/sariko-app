What We Do

A marketplace built for the Filipino diaspora

SariKo is a Progressive Web App marketplace serving the Filipino community in Ho Chi Minh City, Vietnam. Before SariKo, Filipino commerce in HCMC was fragmented across Facebook Groups, Messenger chats, Viber threads, and informal payment arrangements. There was no trusted discovery layer, no buyer protection, and no structured way to pay.

SariKo consolidates all of this into one platform: buyers browse verified Filipino sellers, place orders, pay securely via VNPay, and receive their goods delivered by Lalamove — all without leaving the app.

Platform Architecture

SariKo is Node 1 of Pangea

SariKo is built on Pangea — a node-based commerce infrastructure designed to serve diaspora communities city by city. Each node is a structured marketplace for a specific community in a specific city, governed locally and connected globally.

Our Story

How SariKo began

SariKo was founded on March 6, 2026, in Ho Chi Minh City. The founding insight came from a simple, real experience: trying to find Filipino food for a visiting friend across a maze of Facebook Groups, Messenger threads, and informal contacts — no single place to discover sellers, no trusted way to pay, no reliable delivery.

That experience became the product. Teresa Cantuba Avellaneda of Lutong Quezon in Vietnam became Founding Seller #1. By April 2026, 19 sellers had joined the platform ahead of its public launch on April 18, 2026.

The name SariKo comes from the Filipino word sari-sari — the beloved neighbourhood variety store that is the heartbeat of Filipino community commerce. It is also the Cebuano word for mine — this marketplace belongs to the community it serves.
